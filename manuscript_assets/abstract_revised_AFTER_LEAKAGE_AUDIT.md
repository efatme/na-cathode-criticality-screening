# Revised abstract after leakage audit

Sodium-ion batteries are promising for lower-cost and potentially lower-criticality energy storage, but cathode prioritization requires balancing electrochemical performance, stability, resource constraints, and literature support. Here we present a reproducible, criticality-aware decision-support workflow for sodium-ion cathode screening using computed Materials Project electrode records and text-mined battery literature evidence. Starting from 416 sodium-ion electrode records, 164 passed basic electrochemical screening, 112 were hard-exclusion-free, and 56 satisfied a conservative earth-abundance proxy. Exact formula-level ChemDataExtractor evidence was identified for 18 conservative candidates, while the deduplicated manuscript shortlist contained 95 entries. We compared composition-only, composition-plus-lattice, and post-DFT descriptor protocols under framework/grouped cross-validation and added an explicit feature-leakage audit. After removing Na-stoichiometry near-target features, the post-DFT protocol retained useful performance for voltage and capacity, moderate ranking signal for gravimetric energy, and weak auxiliary signal for stability. Family-level analysis identified phosphate/NASICON-like materials as the strongest conservative candidate pool and transition-metal-oxide-like materials as the strongest text-mined evidence pool. The work is framed as a transparent prioritization workflow rather than experimental validation or cathode discovery.
